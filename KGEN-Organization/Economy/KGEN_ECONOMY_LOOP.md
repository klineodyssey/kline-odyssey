## 1. Purpose

KGEN Economy Loop defines how value moves from Wild Land to civilization-scale markets. It prevents isolated economy features by requiring every economic action to connect to land, buildings, temple services, marketplace activity, KGEN circulation, and civilization growth.

## 2. Full Loop

Wild Land -> Exploration -> Land -> House -> Shop -> Temple -> Exchange -> City -> Civilization -> Civilization War -> Cross-Universe Civilization.

## 3. Wild Land

All land begins as Wild Land. It is not automatically sold by the creator. It becomes usable through exploration, construction, civilization activity, war outcomes, or free market transfer.

## 4. Exploration

Exploration reveals coordinates, resources, risk, distance, and possible civilization value. Exploration must be tied to Universe Map logic and cannot invent a separate universe scale.

## 5. Land

Land can be explored, built, traded, rented, conquered under governance rules, and transferred in free market systems. If NFT support is adopted later, the NFT layer must represent ownership or claim records without replacing Canon.

## 6. House

One land creates one house as its first civilized organ. A house can evolve into a shop, exchange counter, temple service node, warehouse, bank branch, or civic utility.

## 7. Shop

A shop sells goods, App organisms, AI services, DNA packages, equipment, materials, land rights, temple services, and civilization technology. Shop economics must include source, price, risk, and settlement medium.

## 8. Temple

Temple life receives resources, worship, service usage, upgrades, and civilization responsibility. A temple is not only a building; it is a living civilization organ.

## 9. Exchange

11520 花果山交易所 is the civilization financial center and marketplace for App, Land, Temple, AI, DNA, and civilization assets. Exchange rules must protect transparency, listing facts, and market risk disclosure.

## 10. City

A city forms when houses, shops, NPCs, temples, banks, warehouses, and exchange nodes create stable production, trade, and governance.

## 11. Civilization

Civilization grows through technology, rules, public resources, market trust, and defense. Civilization economics must never depend on infinite asset issuance.

## 12. Civilization War

War may change territory or resource control only through governed rules. It cannot become an unbounded mechanism for griefing or hidden asset seizure.

## 13. Cross-Universe Civilization

Cross-universe civilization begins when a mature civilization connects through Portal, Universe Map, and inter-civilization trade rules.

## 14. Risk Rules

Every economy feature must declare inflation risk, market manipulation risk, liquidity risk, user loss risk, and governance mitigation.
