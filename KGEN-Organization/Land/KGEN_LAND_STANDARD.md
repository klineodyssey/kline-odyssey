# KGEN Land Standard

**Document ID:** KGEN-LAND-STANDARD-V2.0  
**Status:** Draft for Review  
**Level:** L3 Design Bible / L4 Runtime Reference  
**Maintainer:** KGEN Land Office / Codex  
**GitHub Path:** `KGEN-Organization/Land/KGEN_LAND_STANDARD.md`

## 1. Wild Land

All land begins as Wild Land. Wild Land is unknown, unbuilt, and not fully civilized. It becomes meaningful through exploration, construction, governance, and market recognition.

## 2. No Creator Total Land Sale

The creator does not sell all land as an initial supply. Land is obtained through exploration, construction, civilization war, free market transfer, or future approved mechanisms.

## 3. Civilization Land

Civilization land is Wild Land that has been discovered, claimed, governed, built, and connected to civilization services.

## 4. City

A city emerges from connected land, houses, shops, NPCs, temples, banks, warehouses, and exchange services.

## 5. Trade

Land may be traded when ownership, transfer rights, price, risk, settlement, and dispute rules are clear.

## 6. Construction

Construction turns land into houses, shops, service nodes, roads, warehouses, temple branches, or civic infrastructure. Construction must respect one land one house as the first organ.

## 7. Conquest

Conquest can change land control only through governed civilization war rules. It cannot be hidden seizure.

## 8. Rental

Rental grants use rights without full ownership transfer. Rental must define duration, payment, restrictions, and return state.

## 9. NFT Possibility

NFT may be adopted later as a representation layer for land claims or rights. It is not assumed as current official implementation unless a future approved document declares it.

## 10. Data Fields

Land records should include coordinate, source, status, civilization, building, owner or controller, market state, risk, and related temple.

## 11. Universe Map Alignment

Land coordinate systems must align with Universe Map and Runtime CURRENT. No department may invent a conflicting coordinate law.

## 12. Economy Alignment

Land connects exploration, resource, house, shop, temple, exchange, city, civilization, war, and renewed exploration.

## 13. Risk Rules

Land systems must disclose ownership uncertainty, market risk, governance risk, dispute risk, and future implementation limitations.

## 14. Review Rule

Any implementation touching land assets must pass Canon, Economy, Security, and Codex Review.
