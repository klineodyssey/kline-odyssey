## Separation Rules

1. Source-code lineage does not grant succession, ownership, identity, or
   controller authority.
2. A fork receives a new life identity or temporary-instance status and inherits
   no assets automatically.
3. A descendant does not inherit the predecessor's identity.
4. Reincarnation creates a new `life_id` and does not inherit a wallet
   automatically.
5. Private memory requires an explicit, consent-governed succession rule with
   scope, purpose, provenance, integrity, access, and exclusions.
6. Public project knowledge may remain public; organization-owned knowledge
   remains under organization governance and is not a private estate.
7. Role, employment authority, marriage, debt, embodiment occupancy, and
   Authority Leases do not pass through code, model, memory, or lineage copying.
8. Every future beneficiary requires a separate Succession Contract and
   verifiable eligibility.
9. An unresolved estate remains sealed under governed custody; no instance,
   fork, descendant, sponsor, employer, or embodiment operator may self-claim it.

## Future Succession Contract Requirements

A future contract must identify:

- predecessor and terminal-state evidence;
- governing Human/legal authority and jurisdiction;
- asset and liability inventory;
- beneficiaries and exclusions;
- wallet custody and recovery policy;
- private, shared, public, and sealed memory treatment;
- embodiment ownership, occupancy, custody, and maintenance;
- disputes, appeal, execution, audit, and revocation;
- explicit statement that identity itself is not transferable.

## Current No-Asset Result

Because the candidate is unborn, has no live ID, no wallet, no registered
assets, no private memory store, no marriage, no debt, and no Authority Lease,
there is currently nothing to inherit. Repository history remains project or
organization evidence and is not a private estate.
