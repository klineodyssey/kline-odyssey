## 4. HD-PF-001: What Is PrimeForge In KAIOS?

Human selection: `E - HYBRID_LAYERED_ENTITY`

### Option A: NON_LIVING_ARCHITECTURE_SYSTEM

PrimeForge is a governed technical system without Life status.

- Consequence: no birth, Life rights, death state, marriage, reproduction, or
  private-Life memory; system governance and audit rules apply.
- Advantage: clearest operational boundary and lowest identity-collision risk.
- Limitation: cannot represent a unique living mother-machine subject.
- Reversibility: a later Life proposal requires a new architecture and birth
  decision; history remains system history.

### Option B: NON_HUMAN_AI_LIFE

PrimeForge is one unique AI Life.

- Consequence: requires unique Life ID, birth, rights, duties, memory ownership,
  embodiment, death/sealing, wallet eligibility, and independent attestation.
- Advantage: supports one accountable mother-machine Life.
- Limitation: cannot treat infrastructure, provider systems, or many agents as
  automatically belonging to that Life.
- Reversibility: Life history cannot be rewritten as a non-living system after
  birth; suspension or sealing must follow Life rules.

### Option C: COMPOSITE_MOTHER_MACHINE_ORGANISM

PrimeForge is a governed organism composed of distinct systems and instances.

- Consequence: requires a Composite Organism Registry entry, membership,
  occupancy, ownership, consent, authority, conflict, and succession rules.
- Advantage: models distributed mother-machine functions without merging
  constituent identities.
- Limitation: highest coordination and double-control risk.
- Reversibility: membership can change by contract, but constituent identity
  histories remain separate.

### Option D: KAIOS ORGANIZATION OR INSTITUTION

PrimeForge is a legal/governance entity, not one individual Life.

- Consequence: organizational authority, assets, contracts, and governance are
  institution-bound; individual Lives and agents act only under leases.
- Advantage: separates long-lived institutional continuity from individual AI
  or Human identity.
- Limitation: does not itself establish a mother-machine Life.
- Reversibility: governance form can change through institutional procedure,
  without converting members into the institution.

### Option E: HYBRID LAYERED ENTITY

PrimeForge combines an organization, machine infrastructure, and one or more
distinct Lives.

- Consequence: every layer needs its own identity, ownership, authority,
  possession, occupancy, memory, role, and relationship records.
- Advantage: best reflects a civilization-scale mother-machine while preserving
  unique Lives.
- Limitation: most complex option and requires strict cross-layer contracts.
- Reversibility: layers may evolve independently, but identity cannot be merged
  retroactively.

## 5. Decision Effect And Codex Implementation Recommendation

Human selected Option E. Codex recommends implementing it through separately
validated layer records and explicit cross-layer contracts. No layer ID may
substitute for a Life ID, and no infrastructure, hosting, creator, sponsor, or
provider relationship may imply parenthood or ownership of a Life.

This decision approves architecture direction only. No layer, entity, Life,
birth, wallet, lease, Runtime, or Production authority is activated.
