# DNA License Standard

## 1. Role Separation

Every blueprint distinguishes `creator`, `owner`, `species_owner`, `individual_owner`, `license_holder`, `operator`, `reviewer` and `manufacturer`. One actor may hold multiple roles only when declared; no role implies another.

## 2. License Classes

- `PRIVATE_USE`
- `PERSONAL_USE`
- `COMMERCIAL_USE`
- `RESEARCH_USE`
- `FARM_USE`
- `BREEDING_USE`
- `DERIVATIVE_ALLOWED`
- `DERIVATIVE_FORBIDDEN`
- `ROYALTY_LICENSE`
- `OPEN_CREATION_LICENSE`
- `K11520_LISTING_ELIGIBLE`

A license states jurisdictional disclaimer, scope, territory/realm, duration, users/instances, derivative rights, sublicensing, confidentiality, revocation, termination, audit, maintenance, fee, royalty and dispute route.

## 3. Royalty Rules

A `RoyaltyRule` records event type, unit, gross basis, fixed/percentage formula, cap, floor, beneficiaries, rounding, refund treatment, expiry and audit evidence. Total distribution must equal 100 percent of the designated royalty pool. It cannot secretly tax unrelated transactions.

## 4. Pricing Models

Supported architecture models are fixed license, usage subscription, per-instance fee, maintenance subscription, revenue share, research grant and open creation. Financial claims remain prototype ledger intents and do not promise token or fiat value.

## 5. K11520 Compatibility

A DNA-related listing requires life/species ID, owner authority, blueprint revision, approved public hash, lineage, integrity/quality/balance status, performance evidence scope, license offered, rights offered, royalty rule, maintenance duty, risk disclosure, valuation method and listing status.

Listings distinguish use, license, revenue share, maintenance, governance, organ subscription and complete ownership rights. `K11520_LISTING_ELIGIBLE` is only an eligibility flag; it is not approval, legal security status or automatic settlement.

## 6. Private Material

A license never exposes a CONFIDENTIAL, HEAVEN_SECRET or DIVINE_VAULT payload. Public marketplace records use safe metadata, approved hashes and access-controlled delivery references.
