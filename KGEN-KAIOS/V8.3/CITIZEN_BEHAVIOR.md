# Citizen Behavior

**Document ID:** KAIOS-V8.3-CITIZEN-BEHAVIOR  
**Status:** Draft for Review / Prototype

## 1. Purpose

Citizen Behavior defines how a civilization lifeform moves through time. Citizens are not players. They are living civilization entities that work, consume, rest, learn, trade and upgrade.

## 2. Actions Per Tick

Each Citizen may perform:

- Work.
- Consume.
- Rest.
- Move.
- Learn.
- Trade.
- Upgrade.

## 3. Behavior Inputs

- Citizen Time.
- Profession.
- Residence.
- Employer.
- Business state.
- Energy.
- Health.
- Inventory.
- Wallet reference.
- Reputation.
- Temple activity.
- Market demand.

## 4. Behavior Outputs

- Wage signal.
- Consumption event.
- Energy change.
- Skill change.
- Reputation change.
- Transaction record.
- Governance signal.

## 5. Energy and Fatigue

Citizen energy decreases with work, movement and trade. Energy recovers through rest, residence safety and temple support. A citizen with low energy should reduce productivity before creating negative governance signals.

## 6. Upgrade

Upgrade is allowed when a citizen has enough experience, energy, skill, reputation and compatible profession path. Upgrade never rewrites Canon and never creates real economic rights by itself.
